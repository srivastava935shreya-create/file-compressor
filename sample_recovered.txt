Huffman coding is a particular type of optimal prefix code that is commonly used for lossless data compression.
The process of finding or estimating such a code proceeds by means of Huffman's algorithm.
This algorithm was developed by David A. Huffman while he was a Ph.D. student at MIT, and published in 1952.
Binary trees and priority queues are two fundamental data structures used in this algorithm.
